## Architecture Changes

- Recovery planning is now a first-class control-plane capability.
- Recovery Coordinator owns recovery plan creation and planning decisions.
- Scheduler remains the owner of node selection.
- ReservationManager remains the owner of capacity holds.
- Migration store records recovery intent without executing migration.
- Event Bus and Observability continue to persist recovery decisions to the timeline.

## Known Limitations

- Recovery Coordinator never executes workloads.
- Recovery Coordinator does not call runtime interfaces.
- Recovery plans are manually/API-created; no automatic recovery trigger exists.
- Planned migration records do not yet execute movement, backup transfer, restore, or runtime actions.
- Recovery plan creation is not fully transactional across plan, item, migration, and reservation records.
- Reservation expiration remains API-process-local.

## Risks

- Future Failover Engine must not bypass RecoveryPlan state and recreate its own planning logic.
- Future execution needs per-server recovery locks to prevent multiple active plans from acting on the same workload.
- Restore ownership remains undefined.
- Runtime migration contracts still do not support real migration execution.

## Next Exact Step

Review Phase 13 and `docs/FAILOVER_ENGINE_READINESS.md`. If approved, begin a Failover Engine Foundation phase that consumes recovery plans but still avoids live migration, backup transfer, restore execution, and automatic recovery until those boundaries are explicitly designed.
